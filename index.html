Brand Manifesto
— Written in full
001
Most clothing asks nothing of you.
We ask everything.
There is a kind of dressing that is purely functional. You put something on, you leave the house, no one notices, not even you. We are not interested in that. We believe a garment should demand something from the person wearing it — attention, intention, a point of view. Not effort. Just awareness.
002
We build from the inside out.
Every piece starts as a structural problem. Proportion. Weight. How it falls. Where it opens. What it does when you move. We approach garment design the way an engineer approaches a bridge — form follows intention, and every detail earns its place or doesn't exist at all. There is no decoration for its own sake here. Only decisions.
003
The garment ends.
The wearing begins.
We design a foundation — deliberate, resolved, precise. But we don't design how you wear it. That's yours. Layer it. Leave it open. Tuck it in a way we never intended. Two people can own the same piece and it will never look the same twice. The design is fixed. The expression is not. That gap — between what we made and what you make of it — is where Objects of Desire lives.
004
Desire is not self-made.
It is given by others.
The name is deliberate. We are not interested in clothing that flatters you when you look in the mirror. We are interested in clothing that makes the person across the room lean toward whoever you're with and ask, quietly — where did they get that? That question is the product. That moment of covetability — unhurried, unannounced, impossible to manufacture — is what we are building toward with every piece we release.
005
Nothing random.
Nothing repeated.
We live in an era of infinite clothing and almost no garments worth keeping. Fast decisions, cheap executions, and designs that were never truly designed — just assembled from what was already there. We refuse that entirely. Every Objects of Desire piece is a considered act. Made to be owned for years. Made to look like nothing else in the room. That is not a promise. It is the only way we know how to work.

About Us
Who We Are

Founded in Indonesia.
Built for the world.

Objects of Desire was founded on a single, unresolved frustration: that dressing well in Indonesia — truly well, not just expensively — meant either settling for something generic or overpaying for something imported that was never designed for you.

We built this brand to close that gap. Not by copying what exists. By deciding what should.

Every collection is built around a design logic that borrows from engineering more than it borrows from fashion. We ask structural questions first — how does this piece hold itself? What does it do when worn? Where does it create tension and where does it release? The aesthetic answers those questions. Not the other way around.

The result is clothing that reads as effortless but rewards attention. Pieces that feel considered without announcing themselves. Garments that become more interesting the longer you look at them — and more personal the longer you wear them.

We are not interested in the loudest voice in the room. We are interested in the person everyone is quietly watching. That is who we design for. That is who Objects of Desire is for.

I
Structural Intention
Structure before surface. Every proportion, seam, and silhouette is a decision — not a default. We build garments the way a good building is built: from the logic of the form outward.
II
Covetable Singularity
Desire is not manufactured by a logo. It is provoked by a piece that looks like nothing else — on the rail, and especially on the person. We design to be coveted. That is the entire brief.
III
Open-Ended Wearing
The piece is resolved. How it's worn is not. We leave room in every garment for the person to finish it — layered, adapted, inhabited differently than anyone else ever would.
The Name
Objects
What we make
Each piece is treated as a designed object — not a product, not a unit, not an SKU. Constructed with the same intention a sculptor brings to form. You don't buy a shirt. You acquire something. The distinction matters to us, and eventually it will matter to you.
Desire
What we provoke
Not vanity. Not self-approval. The specific feeling created in others when they see what you're wearing and want it for themselves — quietly, involuntarily. That reaction is what every piece is designed to produce. The brand name is the promise.
